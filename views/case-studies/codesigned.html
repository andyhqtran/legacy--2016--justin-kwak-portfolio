CODESIGNED

Modernization of Intranet Solution for Enterprises

Utilization of SharePoint to create a highly customized Intranet solution for enterprises

About the Project

Codesigned offers custom Intranet solutions powered by Sharepoint, Microsoft’s team collaboration software, to enterprise companies ranging from 500 to as large as 10,000 employees.

They wanted to create a clean and simple Intranet platform to offer to their clients, in which then they can later scale with additional plugins and add-ons.


Research

The first thing I did was interview total of 8 current clients of Intranet. They were 1 hour user interview sessions, ranging from regular employees to executives in corporate communications.

Throughout this process, I was able to gain insight from one of the largest clients from Codesigned on how employees utilized their current Intranet.


This was incredibly eye opening for me. According to the analytics, 68% of sessions were abandoned within 10 seconds by employees, and 67% of the same cohort did not explore past the home page.

These statistics reaffirmed my belief that the current solution required a lot more than a visual refresh. 

As I’ve projected, peak user engagement was in the morning when people came in for work.

Although these numbers presented above were from one source, they coincided with the information I’ve gathered from almost all users on my interviews.

I then compiled all the data and information collected, and designed sets of user personas.

Make employees feel like they are actually apart of the community, not just bystanders.

Wireframe

Before I began the project, it was crucial for me to learn about the ultimate goal and future aspirations to ensure scalability. We went through multiple iterations of wireframes to reach a solution we all agreed with.

Visuals

Since this is a platform designed for companies to integrate their own brand identity, I intended to have the content drive the product.

From the sales perspective, it is much harder to sell a product that is too bold or unique, hence I tried to stay neutral with my design direction.
